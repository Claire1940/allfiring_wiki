<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering the Layout: Key Areas and Power Positions">
Regardless of the version, understanding the fundamental layout of Firing Range is crucial. The map typically features a central open area, two main lanes flanking it, and various buildings and elevated positions. Each area offers distinct advantages and disadvantages, making map control a dynamic and essential aspect of gameplay.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Control the Central Container Area">
    The central container area is often a hot zone. Controlling this allows you to push into either side lane or cut off enemy rotations. Use the containers for cover but be wary of grenades.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Utilize the Tower (Snipers' Nest)">
    The tower offers a commanding view of the central area and one of the side lanes. It's excellent for long-range engagements but is also a prime target for enemy grenades and focused fire. Use it sparingly and relocate frequently.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Dominate the Office Building">
    The office building provides excellent cover and multiple entry points. Holding the second floor gives you sightlines into the central area and the opposite lane. Be prepared for close-quarters combat inside.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Flank Through the Training Lanes">
    The two side lanes, often featuring a shooting range or obstacle course, are perfect for flanking maneuvers. Use them to bypass direct confrontations and surprise enemies from the side or rear.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Strategic Movement">
Effective movement and map awareness are paramount on Firing Range. Constantly check your mini-map, anticipate enemy pushes, and rotate between power positions to maintain an advantage. Never stay in one spot for too long, as you'll become an easy target.
</Callout>
