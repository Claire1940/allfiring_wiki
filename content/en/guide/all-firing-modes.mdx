### Semi-Automatic Firing Mode

Semi-automatic fire allows you to shoot one round per trigger pull. This mode prioritizes precision and ammunition conservation, making it ideal for engagements where accuracy is paramount. Many competitive players favor semi-auto for its predictable recoil and the ability to land precise headshots. It's the default mode for many weapons and a fundamental skill to master.

| Characteristic | Description | Best Use Cases |
|---|---|---|
| **Rate of Fire** | Controlled by player's trigger speed | Long-range engagements, Headshots, Ammo conservation |
| **Accuracy** | Highest potential accuracy | Sniping, Designated Marksman Rifles (DMRs), Pistols |
| **Ammunition** | Most efficient use of ammo | Sustained engagements, Limited supplies |
| **Recoil** | Manageable per shot | Easy to control, Predictable |

### Full-Automatic Firing Mode

Full-automatic fire allows the weapon to continuously fire rounds as long as the trigger is held down, or until the magazine is empty. This mode excels in close-quarters combat and for suppressing enemies. While it offers high damage output, it also consumes ammunition rapidly and can be challenging to control due to significant recoil.

| Characteristic | Description | Best Use Cases |
|---|---|---|
| **Rate of Fire** | Continuous, highest possible | Close-quarters, Room clearing, Suppression |
| **Accuracy** | Lower due to recoil | Hip-firing, Area denial |
| **Ammunition** | Rapid consumption | Short bursts recommended to conserve |
| **Recoil** | High, difficult to control | Requires practice to manage effectively |

<Callout type="warning">
Using full-auto fire indiscriminately can quickly deplete your ammunition reserves, leaving you vulnerable. Learn to feather the trigger for controlled bursts.
</Callout>

### Burst Firing Mode

Burst fire discharges a predetermined number of rounds (typically two or three) with each pull of the trigger. This mode attempts to strike a balance between the precision of semi-auto and the sustained fire of full-auto. It's effective for medium-range engagements where you need more firepower than a single shot but less wild spray than full-auto. The M16A4 and M4 carbine in real-world scenarios often feature a three-round burst mode.

| Characteristic | Description | Best Use Cases |
|---|---|---|
| **Rate of Fire** | Fixed burst per pull | Medium-range combat, Controlled aggression |
| **Accuracy** | Good, better than full-auto | Engaging multiple targets quickly |
| **Ammunition** | Moderate consumption | Efficient for burst damage |
| **Recoil** | Moderate, manageable | Easier to control than full-auto |

### Ramping Mode

Ramping mode is a dynamic firing system often seen in paintball games or certain futuristic shooters. It starts with semi-automatic fire, but if you maintain a consistent trigger pull rate (e.g., three balls per second), the system automatically "ramps up" the rate of fire to a much higher, pre-set limit (e.g., 10.5 balls per second). This allows players to achieve high rates of fire without constantly holding down the trigger, offering a unique blend of control and aggression.

| Feature | Description | Strategic Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **Initial Fire** | Semi-automatic | Precision, Ammo conservation |
| **Ramp-Up Condition** | Consistent trigger pulls (e.g., 3bps) | Natural transition to higher fire rate |
| **Max Fire Rate** | Pre-set limit (e.g., 10.5bps) | Sustained suppression, Increased damage |
| **Control** | Player-initiated, board-assisted | Allows for both single shots and rapid fire |

<Callout type="success">
Ramping mode is excellent for aggressive players who value both initial precision and the ability to quickly transition into sustained fire during an engagement.
</Callout>

### Select Fire Systems in Historic and Modern Weapons

The concept of "select fire" – the ability to switch between different firing modes – has been a critical feature in firearms development since the late 19th century. Early examples like the Cei-Rigotti and the M1918 Browning Automatic Rifle (BAR) incorporated these capabilities, allowing soldiers to adapt their weapons to various combat situations. Modern assault rifles like the AK-47, M16, and FN FAL continue this tradition, offering versatility on the battlefield.
