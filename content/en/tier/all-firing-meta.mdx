<Section icon={Target} title="The Process of Workforce Transformation">
The transition to an AI-augmented workforce involves several critical steps, not just simply replacing humans with machines. It's a complex process of identifying tasks suitable for automation, investing in AI infrastructure, and reskilling the remaining workforce.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Task Identification and Automation">
    Companies first identify routine, repetitive tasks that can be efficiently handled by AI. This often includes data entry, basic customer support, code generation, and HR functions. AI tools are then deployed to automate these processes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Investment in AI Infrastructure">
    Billions are poured into developing and acquiring advanced AI models, computing power, and specialized talent. This includes hiring AI engineers, data scientists, and researchers to build and maintain these new systems.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Workforce Restructuring and Reskilling">
    Layoffs occur in roles made redundant by AI. Simultaneously, companies invest in reskilling programs for existing employees, focusing on skills that complement AI, such as AI oversight, complex problem-solving, and creative tasks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Optimizing AI-Augmented Teams">
    The goal is to create lean, highly efficient teams where humans work collaboratively with AI tools. This allows for greater productivity with fewer personnel, leading to significant long-term cost savings and innovation.
  </Step>
</Steps>
