### Rozeul: The Thunderous Dominator

Rozeul stands as a **T0 Main DPS** due to his unparalleled consistency and damage output. His kit effectively combines fast AoE clearing with excellent single-target damage, making him suitable for almost all content types, from clearing waves of enemies to taking down formidable bosses.

<Callout type="success" title="Rozeul's Versatility">
Rozeul's true strength lies in his flexibility. He seamlessly integrates into mono-Thunder teams, hybrid setups, boss-focused compositions, and even budget lineups without a significant drop in effectiveness. This universal value is a rare asset, especially at launch.
</Callout>

### Sion: The Free Utility Queen

Sion is a **T0 Support/Sub-DPS** character, and remarkably, she is obtained for free early in the game. Her Wind utility is crucial for maintaining combat flow, especially when paired with burst DPS units like Rozeul. Many players consider her the "glue" unit, making almost any team composition smoother and more efficient.

| Character | Element | Role | Rarity | Availability | Key Features |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Rozeul** | Thunder | Main DPS | 6★ | Standard Banner | High consistent damage, AoE + ST, adaptable |
| **Sion** | Wind | Support/Sub-DPS | 6★ | Free | Excellent utility, energy generation, team enabler |

### Ilscha: The High-Investment Ice Queen

Ilscha is a **T1 Burst DPS** with the potential to reach T0 status, but her ranking is heavily dependent on investment. At base level, she is a strong DPS, but with her signature weapon and a dedicated Ice team, her damage skyrockets. This makes her a luxury investment, often more expensive than Rozeul for similar top-tier performance.

### Felgia: Reliable Wind Sustained DPS

Felgia, a **T1 Sustained DPS**, offers great synergy with Sion, forming a comfortable dual-Wind setup that is both fast and reliable. She excels in longer combat scenarios where enemies endure multiple rotations. Unlike Ilscha, Felgia scales steadily and performs well even with average gear, making her a safer long-term investment.

### Kirika: The Practical Fire DPS

Kirika fills the role of a **T1 Fire DPS**, providing essential elemental coverage, particularly against Ice enemies. Her ability to easily integrate into mixed teams as a flex DPS without disrupting team structure makes her incredibly practical. Fire coverage remains useful throughout the game, making her a solid choice.
