### Soft Pity vs. Hard Pity

Understanding the difference between soft and hard pity is crucial for managing your expectations and resources.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Soft Pity">
    - **Definition**: The range of pulls where your chances of getting a 5-star item significantly increase, even before hitting the hard pity threshold.
    - **All Firing**: While not explicitly stated as "soft pity" in the game's description, the underlying gacha mechanics often increase rates after a certain point (e.g., after 60-70 pulls) to make hitting the hard pity less frequent.
    - **Strategy**: This is where you might consider doing single pulls if you're close to hard pity to conserve resources.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Hard Pity">
    - **Definition**: The absolute maximum number of pulls you can make without receiving a 5-star item. Once you hit this number, a 5-star is guaranteed.
    - **All Firing**: For character and weapon banners, this is typically **80 pulls**. Once you reach 80 pulls without a 5-star, your next pull will be a 5-star.
    - **Strategy**: Always aim to have enough resources to hit hard pity if you are targeting a specific character or weapon.
  </Card>

<Section icon={Target} title="Navigating the 50/50 System">

The 50/50 system is a common feature in gacha games, and **All Firing** is no exception for its rate-up banners. This system determines whether your guaranteed 5-star pull will be the featured character/weapon or a standard pool item.

### How the 50/50 Works

When you perform pulls on a featured character or weapon banner in All Firing, and you hit a 5-star:
- There is a **50% chance** that the 5-star will be the featured character/weapon on the banner.
- There is a **50% chance** that the 5-star will be a standard character/weapon from the general pool.

<Callout type="warning" title="Losing the 50/50">
If you lose the 50/50 and receive a standard character/weapon, your next 5-star pull on *any subsequent featured banner of the same type* is **guaranteed** to be the featured character/weapon. This "guarantee" carries over between banners, making it a crucial mechanic for long-term planning.
</Callout>

### 50/50 Outcomes and Strategies

| Scenario | Outcome | Next 5-Star Status | Recommended Action |
| :------- | :------ | :----------------- | :----------------- |
| **Win 50/50** | Get Featured Unit | 50/50 Resets | Decide if you want duplicates or save for next banner. |
| **Lose 50/50** | Get Standard Unit | Guaranteed Featured | Continue pulling on the current banner if you want the featured unit, or save for a future desired unit. |

### Don't "Build Pity" on Unwanted Banners

Some players attempt to "build pity" on banners featuring characters they don't want, hoping to lose the 50/50 and secure a guaranteed featured character on a future banner. This strategy is highly risky.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Why It's Risky">
    - **Accidental Wins**: You might accidentally win the 50/50 and get a character you don't want, resetting your guaranteed status.
    - **Wasted Resources**: The resources spent on an unwanted banner could have been saved for a truly desired character, potentially delaying your acquisition.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Recommended Approach">
    - **Pull Only for Desired Units**: Only pull on banners that feature characters or weapons you genuinely want.
    - **Save for Guarantee**: Focus on saving enough currency to hit hard pity (and potentially lose and win the 50/50) on banners you care about.
  </Card>
