In many role-playing games, companions are integral to the player's journey, offering combat support, unique quests, and engaging dialogue. However, there might come a time when you wish to streamline your party or simply go solo. Knowing how to go about firing all companions can be crucial for various gameplay styles, from challenge runs to simply managing your roster more effectively. This guide will walk you through the general methods and considerations for dismissing your allies across different gaming titles in 2026.

<Section icon={Target} title="Understanding Companion Dismissal Mechanics">

The process of firing all companions isn't always straightforward and can differ significantly between games. Some titles offer a clear "dismiss" option, while others might hide it behind specific dialogue choices or even require certain in-game locations. It's essential to understand these mechanics to avoid unintended consequences or losing a valuable ally permanently.

<Callout type="warning" title="Permanent Decisions">
Be aware that in some games, firing a companion can be a permanent decision, meaning they cannot be re-recruited later. Always save your game before making significant party changes.
</Callout>

### Step-by-Step Dismissal

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initiate Conversation">
    Approach the companion you wish to dismiss on your ship, the *Unreliable*, or in any other location where they are active.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Select Dialogue Option">
    Engage them in conversation and look for dialogue options related to their service or future with the crew. Typically, there will be a clear option to "ask them to leave" or "terminate their employment."
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Confirm Dismissal">
    The companion will usually express their surprise or disappointment, and you'll have to confirm your decision. Once confirmed, they will depart.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="SAM Unit Specifics">
    For SAM units, the process is similar but involves confirming the termination of their employment contract through a specific prompt.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Re-recruitment">
In *The Outer Worlds*, dismissed companions can generally be re-recruited later by finding them at their original recruitment location or a designated hub area. Their loyalty and quests might be affected, but they are not permanently gone.
</Callout>

*Mount & Blade II: Bannerlord* has a slightly less intuitive system for companion management, especially when it comes to dismissal. Players often find themselves searching for the correct method.

### The Bannerlord Approach

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Initial Misconception">
    Many players initially try to dismiss companions by talking to them in taverns or within cities, which often doesn't present the "fire" option.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Correct Method">
    The key is to **talk to the companion when you are outside of a city or settlement**, while they are part of your active party. This makes the dismissal option available.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

### Companion Dismissal Steps

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Form Your Party">
    Ensure the companion you wish to fire is currently in your active party. If they are not, tell them to join your party first.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Travel Outside a City">
    Leave any city, town, or settlement and enter the world map. This is crucial as the option might not appear indoors.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Initiate Dialogue">
    Right-click on the companion's thumbnail in your party screen or directly speak to them on the world map.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Select Dismissal Option">
    Within the dialogue options, you should now find the choice to "let them go" or "give them the boot." Confirm your decision.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Encyclopedia Entry">
In *Mount & Blade II: Bannerlord*, some players have reported that fired companions might appear as "dead" in the in-game encyclopedia. This is generally a visual bug or a way the game handles their removal from active play, not necessarily an indication of their actual demise.
</Callout>
