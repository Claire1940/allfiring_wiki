<Section icon={Swords} title="Understanding Lyra Firing Animations">
Lyra's weapon firing animations can be a source of frustration, particularly when they don't replicate as expected across different players in a multiplayer environment. The core of these animations often lies within the Gameplay Ability System (GAS). Specifically, the `GA_Weapon_Fire` ability is responsible for initiating the firing sequence, which includes playing the associated animation montage. However, developers frequently report inconsistencies, such as only the instigating player or the server observing the animation, while other clients see nothing.

<Callout type="warning" title="Common Replication Pitfall">
A common issue is that firing animations for certain weapons (like the shotgun in the Lyra starter project) might not replicate to all clients, appearing broken to non-instigating players. This often stems from subtle differences in how replication is handled for different weapon types or incorrect animation rate settings.
</Callout>

To effectively troubleshoot, it's essential to understand the distinction between how a pistol's firing animation might replicate flawlessly, while a shotgun's does not. This often points to specific configuration differences or replication logic within the weapon's associated Gameplay Ability.

### Step-by-Step Debugging Process

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Verify Gameplay Ability Execution">
    First, confirm that the `GA_Weapon_Fire` ability is indeed running on the instigating client and the server. For multiplayer, it's crucial to understand that the ability might not run directly on non-instigating clients.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Inspect `PlayMontageAndWait` Calls">
    Locate where `PlayMontageAndWait` is called within your weapon's `GA_Weapon_Fire` ability. This function is responsible for playing the animation montage. Ensure it's configured to replicate correctly.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Check Animation Auto Rate">
    A common fix for the shotgun animation issue is to adjust the `Auto Rate` parameter. If it's set too high (e.g., `2.2`), the animation might play too fast for some clients to register, leading to it appearing broken.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Compare Working vs. Broken Weapon Abilities">
    If one weapon's firing animation works (e.g., pistol) and another's doesn't (e.g., shotgun), carefully compare their respective `GA_Weapon_Fire` abilities and associated animation assets. Look for any subtle differences in settings, replication flags, or montage configurations.
  </Step>
</Steps>
